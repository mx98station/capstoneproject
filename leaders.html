Joseph Stalin
Joseph Stalin ruled the Soviet Union for more than two decades, instituting a reign of terror. Stalin forced rapid industrialization in the Soviet Union, and the collectivization of agricultural land, resulting in millions dying from famine, while others were sent to camps. Despite this, his Red Army helped defeat Nazi Germany during WWII.

Adolf Hitler
Adolf Hitler was the leader of Germany when World War II began, and led Germany through the war. As leader of the Nazi Party in Germany, he oversaw policies that resulted in the deaths of millions of people. His policies caused the genocide carried out against Jewish communities, known as the Holocaust.

Benito Mussolini
As prime minister of Italy, Mussolini joined forces with Adolf Hitler, entering the war in 1940, on the side of the Nazis. In 1943, the allies invaded Italy, and in 1945, Mussolini was executed.
